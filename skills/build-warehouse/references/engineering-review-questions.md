# Engineering Review Questions

Use these questions after creating an initial brief or conceptual model. They
are intended for the responsible engineer.

## Project Basis

- What is the project location and wind design basis?
- Which standards and editions should govern the work?
- What is the building use, risk category, expected lifespan, and fire
  requirement?
- What deliverables are required: concept model, approval drawings,
  fabrication drawings, mounting drawings, material takeoff, or all of them?

## Geometry And Systems

- Confirm span, length, eave height, ridge height, roof slope, and bay spacing.
- Confirm typology: portal frame, truss/tesoura, shed, geminated span, or crane
  bay.
- Confirm roof and wall cladding types and their support spacing.
- Confirm doors, gates, windows, lanternim, skylights, gutters, and downspouts.
- Confirm braced bays and any openings that conflict with bracing.

## Loads And Serviceability

- Confirm permanent, roof live, wind, equipment, suspended, crane, and solar
  loads.
- Confirm serviceability limits for vertical deflection, lateral drift, roof
  ponding, cladding, and crane operation.
- Confirm whether second-order/stability effects must be included in the
  calculation model.

## Materials And Profiles

- Confirm steel grade and supplier preference.
- Confirm allowed profile families: Gerdau W/HP, U, cantoneiras, tubes, welded
  I/H, cold-formed purlins/girts, or built-up members.
- Confirm if preliminary member sizes are engineer-provided or should remain
  placeholders.

## Connections And Foundation Interface

- Confirm base assumptions: pinned, fixed, or pending.
- Confirm anchor/chumbador concept and concrete interface assumptions.
- Confirm connection philosophy: shop welded, field bolted, moment joints,
  flexible shear joints, gusset details, and splices.
- Confirm any office-standard details or supplier-standard details to reuse.

## Durability And Construction

- Confirm exposure environment and corrosion protection strategy.
- Confirm paint/galvanizing requirements, surface preparation, and inspection.
- Confirm fabrication split, shipping constraints, maximum piece length, and
  erection constraints.
- Confirm whether temporary bracing, lifting points, or mounting sequence must
  be modeled or documented.
